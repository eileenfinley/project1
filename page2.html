What is Ultimate Frisbee?

The Basic Rules

Ultimate Frisbee is played between two teams, each aiming to score points by catching the Frisbee in the opposing team's end zone. The game is non-contact, and here are the basic rules:

  1. Starting Play: The game begins with a "pull," where one team throws the Frisbee to the other, initiating play.
  2. Movement: Players can't run while holding the Frisbee but may pivot on one foot to change direction. The defending team marks the thrower.
  3. Scoring: Points are scored by catching the Frisbee in the opposing team's end zone, similar to a touchdown in football.
  4. Turnovers: If the Frisbee is dropped, intercepted, or goes out of bounds, possession transfers to the other team.
  5. Fouls: Players must avoid physical contact, and fouls can be contested or resolved by those involved.
  6. Stall Count: The thrower has a limited time (usually ten seconds) to make a pass. If they don't, it's a turnover.
  7. Self-Officiation: Players are responsible for their own actions on the field and resolve disputes amicably, promoting the "Spirit of the Game."
